Swainsons Thrush (Catharus ustulatus)

 

Swainson's Thrush - Johnson, Tim 1

Description:   This medium-sized thrush weighs from 0.8 to 1.6 ounces and is about 6.5 to 7.5 inches long. The Pacific states species is rusty-brown with light brown chest spotting and pale thin eye rings. Male and female are similar while juvenile plumage is spotted above and below.

 

Migration & Habitat:  Their summer grounds are in coniferous forests. In the Willamette Valley they live in mixed woods and riparian areas. They winter in tropical forests of Central America.

Breeding range: Green. Winter range: Red. Range map from, The State of the Mountain Birds Report: 2022. 2

 

Reproduction:   Males establish the breeding territory and the female lays 3-4 eggs in open cup nests in shrub thickets and saplings. Both parents feed the nestlings which fledge about 10-13 days after hatching.

 

Diet:  Their diet is primarily insects and arthropods but also fruits and wild berries. They collect bugs from leaves and chase them on the forest floor.

 

Conservation Status:  There is evidence of decline in Oregon, but their species is classified as “least concern.” With a short breeding season, their greatest threat is habitat loss. During nocturnal migration, they suffer significantly from collisions with windows, towers, and tall buildings.

 

Fun Facts:

  • They are the only North American thrush with an ascending song which is upward-spiraling and flute-like.
  • They are also called a “mosquito thrush” because they snatch insects out of the air mid-flight.
  • There are six different subspecies of Swainson’s thrushes.
  • They often return to the same site to breed each year.
